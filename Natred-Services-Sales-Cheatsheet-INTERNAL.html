Services Sales Cheat Sheet

How to talk about everything Natred sells — what it is, who needs it, and how to explain it without sounding like a brochure.
How to use this: Each service below is broken down the same way — what it actually is (no jargon), who buys it, what pain it solves, and exactly how to bring it up in conversation. You don't need to be technical to sell this. You need to understand the client's problem and point them to the right solution. Lead with the pain, not the product.
01

Complete IT Management

On flyer as "Complete IT Management"
What It Actually Is
We become their IT department. Servers, desktops, laptops, cloud systems, networks — all monitored, patched, updated, and maintained. We handle the day-to-day so their team never has to think about IT unless they want to.
What Problem It Solves
They're either paying an internal IT person who's overwhelmed and reactive, or they've got no one and the MD is playing tech support. Either way, things break and nobody knows until it's a crisis.
Who It's For
Any business with 10–200 staff that doesn't have (or want) a full internal IT team. Especially businesses that have outgrown the "my nephew does our IT" phase. Professional services, medical, retail, logistics, manufacturing.
What They Save
Downtime. The cost of a full-time IT hire. The stress of not knowing if their backups work. Peace of mind that proper monitoring is in place and issues get flagged before they become problems.
Example Scenario
"A 40-person accounting firm has one junior IT guy who's in over his head. Servers aren't patched, backups haven't been tested in months, and every Monday starts with someone's Outlook not working. We take over, stabilise everything, and their team just works."
How to Say It in a Meeting
"How's your IT being handled right now? Do you have someone in-house, or is it a bit of everyone's problem? We take that off the table completely — your team focuses on their actual jobs, and we make sure the tech just works."
02

Microsoft 365 & SharePoint

On flyer as "Microsoft 365 & SharePoint"
What It Actually Is
Full Microsoft 365 setup and management — Exchange Online, Teams, OneDrive, and SharePoint. We specialise in SharePoint implementations built from scratch with proper structure, permissions, and document management from day one. Not migrations of old messes — clean builds that actually work.
What Problem It Solves
Most businesses are paying for M365 but using about 10% of it. Their SharePoint is either empty, a dumping ground, or was "set up" by someone who watched a YouTube video. They're not getting value from what they're already paying for.
Who It's For
Businesses already on M365 that know it could be better, or businesses moving to M365 for the first time. Document-heavy industries: legal, accounting, property, construction, healthcare — anyone drowning in files and folders.
What They Save
Time searching for documents. Version control nightmares. Licence waste from unused or mis-assigned subscriptions. The cost of third-party tools that M365 already does natively.
Example Scenario
"A property management company has files scattered across personal drives, email attachments, and a shared folder on an old server. We build them a SharePoint structure — by property, by document type, with proper permissions — and suddenly everyone can find what they need in seconds."
How to Say It in a Meeting
"You're on Microsoft 365 already, right? How's that going for you? Most businesses we talk to are paying for it but barely using SharePoint or Teams properly. We set that up so it actually works for your team — proper structure, proper permissions, from scratch."

03

VoIP & Teams Calling

On flyer as "VoIP & Teams Calling"
What It Actually Is
We replace their old phone system (PBX, analogue lines, or whatever Telkom setup they're clinging to) with Microsoft Teams as their actual phone system. Real phone numbers, real call routing, voicemail, auto-attendants — all inside Teams.
What Problem It Solves
Their phone system is either ancient, expensive to maintain, or some bolted-on VoIP thing that drops calls. Their team is already on Teams for chat — why are they paying for a separate phone system?
Who It's For
Any business already on M365 that still has a separate phone system. Especially multi-site businesses, remote/hybrid teams, and anyone sick of paying Telkom for lines nobody answers.
What They Save
Monthly line rental and PBX maintenance costs. One less system to manage. Calls work from anywhere — office, home, mobile app. No more "the phones are down" on a Monday morning.
Example Scenario
"A law firm with 3 offices is paying R8k/month for ISDN lines and a PBX that needs rebooting every week. We move them to Teams Calling — same phone numbers, proper call queues for reception, and their lawyers can take calls from their laptops when working from home."
How to Say It in a Meeting
"What are you using for your phone system right now? If your team's already on Teams, there's a good chance you're paying for two systems that do the same thing. We can consolidate that — same numbers, better features, lower cost."
04

Network Infrastructure

On flyer as "Network Infrastructure"
What It Actually Is
We design and install business-grade networks — WiFi, cabling, switches, firewalls, VLANs. We use UniFi gear predominantly because it's enterprise quality without the enterprise price tag, and we can manage it remotely.
What Problem It Solves
Their WiFi drops in the boardroom. The guest network doesn't exist (or is the same as the staff one). The cabling looks like spaghetti. Their network was set up by whoever was cheapest, not whoever knew what they were doing.
Who It's For
Any business moving offices, opening a new site, or fed up with network issues. Retail stores, restaurants, warehouses, offices, medical practices — anywhere people need reliable connectivity.
What They Save
Productivity lost to "the internet is slow" complaints. Security risk from an unsegmented network. The embarrassment of WiFi dropping during a client presentation.
Example Scenario
"A retail chain with 5 stores has consumer-grade routers from their ISP, no VLANs, and POS devices on the same network as the guest WiFi. We install UniFi at each site, segment the networks properly, and manage it all from a central dashboard."
How to Say It in a Meeting
"How's your WiFi and network holding up? A lot of businesses we work with are running on consumer gear that was fine when they had 10 people but can't handle 40. We sort that out properly — business-grade kit, proper segmentation, managed remotely."

Business Intelligence & Data Analytics

On flyer as "Business Intelligence & Data Analytics"
What It Actually Is
We build dashboards and automated reports that pull data from their business systems — accounting software, CRMs, spreadsheets, databases — and turn it into something useful. Think Power BI, custom dashboards, or automated email reports that arrive every Monday morning.
What Problem It Solves
The MD asks "how did last month go?" and someone spends two days building a spreadsheet. Sales data lives in one system, finance in another, ops in a third. Nobody has a single view of the business.
Who It's For
Business owners and execs who want to see their numbers without asking someone to pull a report. MDs, finance directors, ops managers. Especially businesses with data in multiple systems that don't talk to each other.
What They Save
Hours spent building manual reports. Decisions made on outdated or incomplete data. The salary of the person whose main job is "updating the spreadsheet."
Example Scenario

Common Objections & How to Handle Them

"We already have an IT guy / company."
That's great — we're not asking you to fire anyone. But if you're talking to us, something isn't working. We're happy to do a free assessment and give you an honest opinion. If your current setup is solid, we'll tell you.
"We're too small for managed IT."
You're not too small to have problems. If you've got 10+ staff and your business runs on technology, you need someone watching it. We scale to your size — you don't pay for what you don't need.
"How much does this cost?"
It depends on what you need — that's why we start with a free assessment. We'll look at your setup, understand your pain points, and give you a clear proposal with transparent pricing. No surprises, no hidden fees. That's literally our thing.
"We had a bad experience with our last IT provider."
We hear that constantly. That's exactly why transparency is our whole positioning. Every invoice is itemised. Every decision is explained. You'll always know what we're doing and why. And if you're not happy, you can walk — we don't lock anyone into contracts they can't exit.
"Can you just quote on [one specific thing]?"
Absolutely. We don't force you into a managed service deal. Need a network sorted? SharePoint built? Office move handled? We do project work too. Start with one thing, see how we operate, and go from there.
"What makes you different from every other IT company?"
Transparency. Every other IT company says they're good at IT — so do we. The difference is you'll actually understand your invoices, you'll know what you're paying for, and we'll be honest when something is outside our expertise instead of winging it.
